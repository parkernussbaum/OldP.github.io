How can we reduce water waste through responsive web?

Problem

People do not understand their behaviors with water and this leads to waste water and high bills. Water providers do not have the tools to help users save water and understand their water usage behaviors.

Solution: Maya Dashboard

A responsive web dashboard for residential water clients and service providers that helps users visualizing their water usage, create water saving goals, and suggesting tips to reduce usage.

Project Details

When - Fall 2019

Duration - Approx. 6 weeks

Course - Interaction Design Studio, Carnegie Mellon University

Skills - Interaction + UX design

Teammates - Paul Myers and Christine Kim

Context

Water is an essential element of human life and our modern home. An existence without water is unimaginable - how could we cook, clean, or even hydrate ourselves. While the thought of water scarcity is unimaginable here in Pittsburgh - it is becoming increasingly common around the world due to drought, pollution, climate change, and mismanagement. Cities like Pittsburgh cannot take their water supply for granted and that is why it is a problem space focusing on.

Gorilla Research: User Quotes

Early in the project, our team reached out to local Pittsburgh water users, the city of PGH water authority, and city hall for gorilla research on the domain. These are some of their quotes that guided us.

“I don’t care about my water usage... Beacause I do not understand it!"

"What even is normal water usage? It is impossible to know whether I am being excessive or normal."

"Most of our users could not tell you the difference between 10 gallons and 50 gallons .. that is a problem when our goal is to conserve."

Personas based on quotes

Buisness persona - a city council member who encompasses the views of a pro sustainability platform, a tighter civic budget, and wants to lower living costs.

Provider persona - a senior customer service manager who wants to better customer relationship, reduce water waste, and reduce wasted water revenues.

Customer persona - a lower income student who struggles with not understanding water usage, with high water bills, and the overall goal of reducing their usage to lower costs.

Journey mapping

Customer Oppertunity

Customers currently lack a way to tell if their strategies for conserving water are effective - in either the short or long term.

Provider Oppertunity

Providers have no way of understanding customer water usage behaviors, system behvaiors as a whole, and lack content to help users mitigate their water usage.

Storyboarding and Speed-dating concepts

Concept focused on primarily maintaining water behavior records through a household. Preformed poorly.

Concept centered around smart device tracking water through "hearing" the usage. Subjects had privacy concerns and was unpopular.

Selected - Concept focused on helping users visualize their water behaviors and were given suggestions based on their usage patterns.

Lo-fi prototyping in Figma

Our lo-fi mockup emphasized water usage through text, bar graphs and by training a machine learning model to understand usage behavior of users through prompting users with questions. Feedback about the ML model and constant questioning was skeptical - the feeling of surveillance detracted from the ability to learn from the dashboard. We challenged ourselves to provide value with as little disruption to users’ lives as possible, and therefore moved to an insight and visualization based approach.

Home screen of the lo-fi prototype showing a quick link to pay water bill, a simple weekly usage visualization, and a usage insight

User analytic page which had access to more customizable visualizations, quick account summary, and further insights

Questions page prompted users with individualized questions. These would help train a user's ML model to understand their behavior with water

Early exploration of the desktop view for users with all phone screens fitting into one "dashboard" style page. Profile page would grow out of left side if prompted. Navigation was based on phone style with "hamburger" navigation menu. More questions and analytics would be available through this view

Mid-fi prototyping in Figma

The Mid-fi prototype was focused on cleaning up the visuals and adding new functionality to the design. The first allowed users to search and submit tips to reduce water usage by using a tag selection page. The second feature compared users water usage anonymously to other similar households. This comparison replaced the ML model explored in the previous iteration as it was more helpful in giving context to user's behaviors. As well as had the added effect of making users want to decrease their water usage. Feedback of this iteration was overall positive but determined that there was too much text driven data - and that the final iteration should be more visually driven.

Profile page was added to mid-fi and showcased basic user information. Also added a ranking system for similar users in your group

Updated usage page featured scrollable analytics which compared your personal water usage with others in your group - testing found this too text heavy

Insight page evolved into a page where users could search for water reduction tips through popular tags. Page also allowed area for users to submit tips and rate the difficulty and effectiveness

Desktop view became more refined and removed the insights from first dashboard view. Added top of page directory and created a more standard desktop webpage language that matched with user testing heuristic evaluation

Hi-Fi Product: Customer

Moving away from very text heavy display, our hi-fi prototype instead emphasized quick visualizations that are aesthetic and highly legible. Users would be able to very clearly understand their current water usage, the context of that usage in relation to other simal users, and context of that usage in volume. As well as see how their usage is fluctating or an idcation of possible leak in their home. On desktop, users could even explore this data further by setting goals for their usage and use a data slider to dive deeper into usage visualizations. This prototype also devolped the insights page further allowing for a search functionality and a more legible visual language.

Animation showing the design moving from the home page to the data analytics page. Together both pages offer a complete view of users water usage

Desktop view showcased all of the home and analytics page in one quick dashboard view. This view also offered brushing and linking on the data visualization, goal setting, and more data visualization for user to contextualize their water usage.

Animation showing the process users would go through to select tags and the tips they would receive

Desktop view of the same functionality but requiring less animation due to the size and area of pixels

Hi-Fi Product: Provider

With most of our group focus being on the customer, we decide in the end that provider pages should very similarly reflect customer user pages. This being for two reasons: first, that when customers call providers for assistance, they can walk them through the web portal directly, and second, so that both the provider and customers have the access to the same sorts of data. The difference being for the providers web design is that they are able to see customers history in insights and able to see their billing history. Other functionality that the provider view has is the ability to search user's profiles directly, use a map to view any issues in customers properties or water districts, and update any notes on customers’ accounts.

Animation showing how a provider on scene might use the mobile web page to explore nearby properties on the map to find issues in customers’ accounts

Desktop provider view looks similar to customers but has more detailed information on their billing history, insight history, and is able to show if there are any "alerts" on their account. This tool was design specifically for customer service to use when assisting customers with any water usage problems

User search feature on provider's side desktop view allows the customer service agents to at a glance see any potential alerts on user's accounts

Map view allows providers to see any issues in the water line at a water district level. This helps to reduce wastewater and notify providers if there is an issue in a greater neighborhood area

Conclusion

We dubbed our final solution Maya Dashboard becasue Maya is the hebrew name for water - and users/providers alike would be able to easily flow thier data to understand water usage. Overall recpetion of the project was high and as a team we felt a great deal of success. Rather than approach the project from a more gamify or campy angle - we felt that by looking at the billing process our solution was pragamtic and applicable. The project helped me to understand the power of responsive web design as well as reinforced the overall UX design process. It also was a great exploration of figma's animation tools and Dreamweaver for final visualizations.
