Rebreathe: Sustainable air purification and oxygen creation

About

ReBreathe is an exploration into sustainable air purification through the use of Algae and other organic compounds. This project came about during my thesis research into ways to combat indoor air-pollution and sick building syndrome. This project was completed over the course of 16 weeks during my senior year of my industrial education at the University of Houston. Rebreathe helped me to obtain a scholarship through the Make Design Matter foundation, was recognized by the Industrial Design Society of Texas for outstanding research, and also took part in the James Dyson Foundation competition.

Pretext

Indoor air pollution is a silent health crisis that affects a majority of people today. Whether at home or the office, the many manufactured goods that we own today naturally off gas toxic substances. This problem compounded with external pollution factors creates oxygen sinks which causes our naturally inhabited spaces to be lower than ideal. This mix of problems can create serious health repercussions over time.

Exploring the problem space behind indoor air pollution and deoxygenated spaces

Contextualizing how users perceive indoor air pollution, what areas/people are most at a disadvantaged to the problem, and a visualization exploring the many facets relating to each other

Market research exposing the need for low cost filtration replacement and low cost initial purchase in terms of the entire product package. Also a need for natural purification that could create a whole new market of oxygen creation.

Ideation and testing

Early concepts focused on implementing natural plant based filter systems based on Dr.Wolverton's studies at NASA Johnson Space Center in the 1970s. During testing and ideation it seemed that system maintenance / reliability was questionable and a more efficient solution needed to be implemented.

Ideation exploring concepts revolving around a use of hydroponic air filter - as well as images of tests I conducted with hydroponic plant systems

Algae purification

Taking a step back from plants, I decided to explore other forms of naturally purifying life form. Algae is the number one producer of oxygen on the planet earth and is surprisingly robust needing minimal input to stay actively consuming carbon dioxide. Algae mixed with a water medium and activated carbon, can act as an oxygen creator, air purifier, and filtration device. With some testing and early feasibility mockups, I decide to peruse this as my main functional base for Rebreathe.

Visualizations exploring the life cycle of algae and activated carbon

Sketches exploring implementation of an algae based bio reactor system

Prototyping

Early prototypes explored a mix of sizes and scales for the algae purification system. A big portion of the project as well was creating a sustainable functioning system - this was accomplished by programming an array of gas sensors to activate the bioreactor during heavy pollution periods. This data could also be used to help users "see" their air quality.

Visualizations exploring the concept of "seeing" user's air quality - prototypes built with Arduino and in the woodshop exploring the form of the final product

Narrowing down on a final form - pictures exploring the assembly and final production of the Hi-Fi prototype

Final spreads exploring the completed project and multiple "hex" sizes. Each size could be installed in multiple rooms base of need by need basis. Product would be controlled ultimately by a digital asset like a phone application or via web browser
