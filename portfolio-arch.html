Flat pack chair and flat pack dining table made from only wood

Project

Flat packaged furniture products are crucial for the on-demand nature of the economy today and keeping shipping costs down. These pieces were developed for the Infiniti chair design competition exploring aesthetics and assembly with the use of only wood. My vision was that pieces be made from sustainable oak plywood and the fasteners be made from solid oak.

Sketching

Inspiration for the design was pulled from a mix of Nordic style wood working and mid-century modern influences. I was mechanically inspired by kerfing wood and medieval wooden screw assemblies. The final sketches led to an A frame design based on a roof of a house. Thats where the A-frame name comes from.

Refining

Once the final designs were sketched out I went into Rhino and developed some 3D models exploring the many pieces and parts needed to create the design. After running analysis of the structure in Solidworks - I 3d printed the parts and assembled them at home using my lolzbot.

A-frame chair: Final renders and breakdown

The final product consisted of 16 different pieces that could all be flat packed in a standard 4x4 shipping pallet.

Arch Table: Final renders and breakdown

Based on the design of a basic woodshop clamp, the arch table uses 12 pieces to create a table. The piece can be easily assembled and disassembled. It allows for customers to order just the legs or the entire table with the top.
