Orion capsule mockup for Space Center Houston

Project

In the fall of 2016 during my tenure as a contract industrial designer at NASA-Johnson Space Center, I was contracted to assist the Orion public relations department with creating a massive display for Space Center Houston. Space Center Houston, the museum part of Johnson space center, was already displaying an Orion capsule. Yet, it was a completely empty shell. Jesus Garate and I spent the next 4 months working to build out the interior to what it is today. Below are spreads showcasing the work and installation we completed.

Project

Fabrication of the interior was quite an ardeous process. Most of the interior was made of polycarbonitae, aluminum 80/20 substructure, and wood. I was the main fabricator on the team so I spent many weeks creating the pieces to match the spec of the flight worthy Orion capsule.

Final shots of the interior - NASA purchased old ISS space shuttle suits for the opening of the exhibit

Final shots of the interior - the flight seats were left over testing seats from some Human-in-the-loop testing we did at the Habitability Design Center
