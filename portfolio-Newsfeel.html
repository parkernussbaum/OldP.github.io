How can we encourage empathy and prosocial dialouge about current events?

Project Details

When - Spring 2020

Duration - Approx. 8 weeks

Course - Social Web, Carnegie Mellon University

Skills - Interaction + UX design

Teammates - Aaron Bishop, Alica Li, Laura Fulton, Dana Frostig

Role - Lead Designer

Scope

Since the rise of social media networks in the late 2000's, the world has become connected in new and novel ways. However, this increased connectivity has lead users to seek out information, freinds, and social networks that most align with their beliefs and interests. This has caused major problems nationally and internationally due to the rise of increasing partianship belifs, a growing lack of empathy, and social based "filter bubbles". This project attempts to remedy these problems by creating a mobile application for dialouge and many view points about current events.

Dialogue between individuals with different perspectives on current events has devolved and become toxic online.

User's lack diverse opinions outside of their social "filter bubbles"

There is a growing lack of empathy towards differing opinions

Digital social circles tend to be echo chambers of beliefs and ideas

User's lack access to unpartiasn media and content when making their own beliefs

Solution: NewsFeel - A mobile application that allows users to look at current events from multipe persepctives and discuss their beliefs in diverse friendly chatrooms.

Newsfeel gives users the ability to browse and read about current event topics from a range of media sources. After reading top news stories, users can choose to like and dislike them based on their opionons and then do a quick followup questionaire. These results can then be fed into a chat now function which guides users into friendly chat rooms for more personal dialouge promoting empathetic behavior.

Problem space: A majority of users read about current events online and through social media - this leads to biased and filtered perspectives.

Through the use of guerilla research and directed storytelling, we found that a majority of users get their news from social media platfroms via their smart devices. Those who self curate their current event digest, find it increasingly challenging to break from their preferred new sources like the New York Times and Fox news due to confirmation bias. Users feel trapped in their trusted news sources and struggle to find diverese opinions to help them empathize with people who have different beliefs.

“I curate my own news content... it means I have to go out of my way for diverse opinions.”

“There are certain news sources I would never read... I’d rather not waste my time with articles I do not agree with.”

“Sometimes I feel like I live in two separate worlds on Facebook between the Fox and NYT articles... What happened?”

Current solutions do exisit to help with bias and filter bubbles but they focus too much on politics and are not mobile centric .

Most of the product solutions that exis look at exposing bias and political leaning rather then increasing empathy and dialouge. While viewing users own bias can be powerful for identity and transperncy into oneself, it is alone not powerful to help users break from their filter bubbles and empathize. Not only that, most products that do exist today work as either google chrome plugins, standalone news curation websites, or facebook plugins. Our solution will be mobile friendly and leech on users browsing data for bias detection.

To design the perfect chatroom for empathetic dialouge - we created our own chatroom experince with a helpful chat bot to facilitate. Users loved it

With our product form begining to take shape, we wanted to test a core princaple of our application, that is "can a diverse ananomyous chatroom about news stories encoruage empathetic dialouge?" To do this, we selected four diverese particapnts and gave them 3 different articles about Covid-19: one from Fox news, one from the BBC, and one from NPR. From there, we let them join the chat room and I acted like a chat bot to facilitate the converstaion for the enxt half hour.

The Newsfeel Chat Bot Study

Overall, the dialpouge between user subjects was mostly light, fun, and poked at how surreal society was due to Covid-19. While specific facts from the articles were not addressed by particapnts, it was apparent that the information made a difference in the exchange of ideas and story telling. After about 20 minutes in the study, certain particapnts began to open up about how personally challenging things were where they lived and for their families. Below are quotes from users taken after the study was completed.

“Porky really changed my perspective.. since I have lived in the city my whole life it's hard to imagine the effects in rural areas”

“The articles helped frame the discussion... b0ssbabe made an effort to try to be inclusive in her views because of it”

“Talking about it (covid-19) made me feel better... even if we didn't agree on everything it was nice to just decompress”

Hand sketching the flow of the app helped me visualize a customer's journey towards empathy and dialouge with strangers

As the lead designer on the team, I began to sketch on how users journies would begin. From the moment they open the app, to the second a topic catches their eye, to the (hopefully) the life changing conversation they would have in the chat room, it should all be part of the experience. The product itself would be a manifestation of the empethic experince going from awareness, to self education, to finally dialouge.

Lo-fi prototyping and early user testing helped understand how users want to onboard into chatrooms

With an overall flow begining to devolp from topic sleection to chatroom, I wanted to better understand if this exeperince model aligned with what users wanted. By letting a few users pilot out some screens , I found a few crucial pieces of feedback. First, I learned that the article swiping (like tinder) caused hueristic issues and users preferred a more thumbs up/ down approach (incase they wanted to cahnge their minds). Testing also reveled that users wanted at max 3 articles before entering a chat room due to attention span creep. Lastly, that a preview page was crucial at the home browse screen for when users selected a topic. This is becuase large topics like "global warming" can consist of an infinite number of subtopics where users need clarity if it interests them.

Hi-Fi product screens

The final high fidelity product was created as a pitch to the client. It showcased the main features of the application and the aesthetic style we were trying to achieve. It also served as a minimum viable product to display the user flow and test on future users.

Title screen

The title screen shows off the ResMed logo and gives the option to sign up or log in. Users can also change basic settings of the app or ask questions on sign up page before starting.

Creating a new profile

Once users choose to create a new profile, users will be guided through a set of pre screen questions. They serve as a way to help set up users profiles quicker. Not only does it gather basic data about their sleep apnea condition, it also takes that data and recommends products for you based on your answers.

Setting face profile with AR

Once user's are finished with the pre-screen questions, they are prompted to use their phone to capture images of their face. These are used to help determine what size their face is as to proscribe the right mask for them. This then will automatically be updated on their profile and will be auto filled in on future product searching.

Customer's profile

The profile page stores basic customer information, customer's mask/ face information, and their preferences as proscribed from the pre-screen questions. It also shows the life span of current products they own. By tapping the arrows, users will be able to see more details about their products and add replacements to the cart directly.

Product catalog

The dual product layout maximizes the space for products to be shown. It includes filters at the top to find certain products quicker, it automatically shows products based on your recommendations, and lets you know which products have AR visualization/customization. Users can tap the cards to go into the product detail page.

AR customization

The AR page allows customers to swipe between different products and to visualize the products directly on their face. It also gives users the ability to tap different components to customize the colors of the parts. Once they have a selection they like, they can add it directly to their cart and it will take them to the cart review page.

What comes next for Newsfeel? Test, build and refine our chatroom algorthim

We dubbed final solution Fresh Air because we felt given the complicated stakeholder process for patients, this new direct ResMed to patient service would be a "breath of fresh air". Redefining the service ecology for ResMed could potentially yield them more customers, numerous amounts of passive data about customer usage, and more qualitative active data, which could lead to better product solutions. Moving forward, we hope to add more features to the application and roll in other sleep apnea products besides masks and related accessories.
