Mars Acension Vehicle

Project

TThe Mars Ascension vehicle represents an exploratory study into the world of transit systems and minimal habitable volumes for a Mars mission. I worked with the habitability design center to create a sleeping system and flight seats that would provide adequate conditions for four astronauts. These are some samples from the 6 month engagement during my tenure at NASA Johnson Space Center in 2016/2017.

MMSEV vehicle mock up the study was originally focused on - this shell was originally created as a lunar transit vehicle but adopted to the mars mission team

The new mockup being built was eventually where the study migrated. All renders and products created were focused on this alternate body type

Scope

A majority of my contributions for the MAV project was focused on two separate aspects. The first was creating foldable bed/ sleeping infrastructure to accommodate 4 astronauts on a 5 day mission. This design needed to be adaptable, easy to use, and generally easy to test in a human-in-the-loop facility at Johnson Space Center. The second aspect is creating flight type seats for ascension from the Martian surface. Due to the G forces of launce - astronauts need some sort of flight seat to reduce the phycological effects of such a launch.

A page exploring the basis for the project and the goals of my engagment

Flight-Seat study

Using human factors data provided by the USAF and internal studies conducted by HHP - I preformed 2D studies of how 4 astronauts could fit in the MAV interior based on suited 99th percentile dimensions. From there I created a low risk design that utilized studies conducted by the Orion team at Lockheed and aluminum sub structures similar to their flight equipment
